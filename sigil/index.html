The Sigil

A SHA-256 digest is 64 hexadecimal glyphs. The lattice has 64 vertices. A City Key's κ-label — kappa = sha256:H(canonical form) — therefore writes one glyph on every vertex: a rune-row across the whole lattice, the key's identity rendered as a constellation. Sixty-four glyphs, one sigil. Identity is content, not location — derived here, before your eyes, never just asserted.
