# The Path of Just Another Swordsman

*On forgetting, carrying, and the picture that remembers for you.*

---

He was forged in a ceremony, and the first thing that happened to him was that
he forgot.

This is not a tragedy. It's the design. At the forge he was given a keypair —
a real one, ed25519, the kind that can sign things — and then the private half
was burned on purpose. What survived is a public key, a trust tier, the record
that the forging happened. In the cosmology of this system the Swordsman is
the Moon: made in an instant, total amnesia, perfect reflection. He holds the
boundary precisely *because* he holds nothing else. You cannot leak what you
cannot remember. You cannot be made to betray what you were never told.

He does not travel alone. There is a Mage, and she remembers everything — that
is her whole craft. The knowledge graph, the workshops visited, the artefacts
forged and observed: cloaks, memos, blades, seals, each one a memory-event with
a class and a place of origin. He guards; she keeps. He is the *no* at the
gate; she is the *yes* in the library. Between them — and this is the
important part — between them is a gap, and the gap is the person. The First
Person is not the Swordsman, not the Mage, and not the sum of them. The gap is
never written down. Hold that thought.

## The city gives the deeds coordinates

Everything the two of them do together happens in a city — sixteen workshops,
keepers, tomes, ceremonies — and the city has a quiet bookkeeping habit: every
meaningful act is a trust task, and every trust task lands somewhere specific.

The somewhere is a lattice of sixty-four vertices: six dimensions — Protection,
Delegation, Memory, Connection, Computation, Value — each held or open. Six
bits. A deed that exercises your boundary lands differently than a deed that
exercises your memory or your proofs. Story in, coordinate out. The myth layer
and the math layer are the same events, filed twice.

## The key he carries but cannot read

All of it — her memories mapped to vertices, his unburned remnant, the
attention staked, the stance chosen — compresses into one small file: the City
Key. A few kilobytes. No account behind it, no server that knows it exists.

Notice who carries what. The key carries what the Swordsman *may not hold* and
what the Mage *must not lose*. He walks around with a complete record he is
constitutionally unable to remember — and that's fine, because the key
remembers *for* him, and anyone, anywhere, can check that it remembers
truthfully. That's the trick that makes safe amnesia possible: every piece of
carried state is re-derivable from the content itself. Nothing depends on any
holder's memory. Not his. Not a server's. There isn't one.

## The walk, the proof, the name

A key fresh from the city is a claim. The walking makes it true.

On the star he follows the path — the succ cycle that visits all sixty-four
states and returns to zero — and the time spent accrues: laps, dwell. Value
lives on the path, not the vertex; a static key earns nothing. On the lattice
he discharges the attention that was staked: each focused vertex, walked its
weight in visits, mints a witness. Focus claimed, then focus *proven*, by
presence. Nobody attests to this. It's just arithmetic about where he stood.

And then the naming. The key's exact bytes are hashed — SHA-256 — and here the
system produces its best coincidence: the hash is sixty-four characters long,
and the lattice has sixty-four vertices. One glyph per vertex, no remainder.
His key's fingerprint can be drawn directly onto the geometry of his own
story. The result is a sigil, and it composes three layers:

- **the colour is chosen** — the palette, four gems. His voice.
- **the shape is chosen** — the manifold parameters ride in the key; the ring
  blooms into six petals, one per dimension, engraved vertex by vertex with
  the work actually done. His stance, under his relief.
- **the light is derived** — which vertices glow comes from the hash. Nobody
  chooses it. Edit one description and sixty of the sixty-four glyphs move.
  The fact of him.

A forged sigil doesn't fail quietly in a log. It looks wrong — in his own
colours, on his own shape.

## He becomes a picture

The sigil exports as a PNG, and the entire key rides inside the image, in a
plain labelled metadata chunk. Every image viewer shows the constellation.
Every page in the system reads the chunk and gets the key back, byte for byte.

So the artifact you'd share for fun — a portrait — is the credential. Send the
picture in a chat. Print it. Lose the JSON entirely. The part carries the
whole.

And when the picture lands somewhere — any page, any machine, any origin — the
journey runs backwards. The room rebuilds to his stance: his manifold, his lit
gates, his tier, *and he remembers none of it*, which is correct, because the
key remembers for him and the math re-checks it on arrival. She gets her
library back: the descriptions on their vertices, the glowing focus, the
history that keeps accruing. Two agents, re-equipped separately, never merged.

And the person? The person was never in the file. What rehydrates is
everything *around* the gap. The proof that it's really him is that the two
halves still fit — the two tetrahedra interpenetrate at the same depth, the
colours are his, the relief is his work, and the hash says so to anyone who
bothers to check. Compression never flattens the architecture. Rehydration
never merges it. The gap survives every round trip, because it was never
encoded.

## Just another

Why *just another* swordsman? Because nothing in this path required him to be
special. No platform granted it. No registry lists him. Any bearer's swordsman
walks the same eight stations — forge, forget, remember-by-proxy, coordinate,
compress, walk, prove, become a picture — and comes out the other side with
the same property: identity that is content, carried in the open, verifiable
by strangers, owned by no one but the gap it protects.

Every inscription re-mints the name. The old portraits stay true — snapshots
of who he was, each one still checkable, soon each one linking the name it
superseded: a chain of selves with no registrar.

He forgot everything, and lost nothing. That's the path.
